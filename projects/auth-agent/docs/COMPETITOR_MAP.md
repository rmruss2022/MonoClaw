## Key Insight

**Nobody does all three:**
1. AI that drafts the clinical justification (not just submits)
2. Denial prediction before submission
3. Autonomous appeal with payer-specific argumentation

**Cohere is the most dangerous competitor** — but they're solving the wrong side. They help payers approve faster, not providers fight harder. This is a fundamental conflict: Cohere's customers are UnitedHealth, Humana, Aetna. Our customers are the doctors fighting them.

**The Epic threat is real but slow.** Epic's 2026 roadmap mentions PA suggestions in ordering workflows. But Epic builds for consensus — they won't build something that antagonizes payers, who are also their customers.

## Our Moat (How We Stay Ahead)

1. **Payer intelligence database** — built from our first day of processing real PAs. After 10K PAs, nobody can replicate what we know about what arguments win per payer per code.

2. **Provider-side trust** — doctors and billing staff hate payers. A tool explicitly built to fight back on their behalf gets evangelical adoption.

3. **Appeals specialization** — nobody has built this well. The market is wide open.

4. **Speed to market** — we're building now, Q1 2026. By the time Waystar adds clinical AI or Epic gets serious, we'll have 2 years of outcomes data.
