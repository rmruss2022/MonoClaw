SPECIALTY CONTEXT: NEUROLOGY PROCEDURES
(Brain/spine MRI, nerve conduction studies, EEG, EMG)

## Key Clinical Guidelines to Reference
- ACR Appropriateness Criteria for neuroimaging
- AAN Practice Parameters and Clinical Practice Guidelines
- AANEM guidelines for electrodiagnostic studies
- International Headache Society (ICHD-3) classification criteria

## Neurology-Specific Documentation Points

For BRAIN MRI (CPT 70553):
- Headache classification per ICHD-3 criteria
- Duration and frequency of headaches (chronic vs episodic)
- Failed medication trials: list each preventive and abortive medication with dose, duration, and response
- Red flag symptoms: sudden onset, worst headache of life, focal neurologic deficits, papilledema, seizure onset
- Change in headache pattern warranting imaging
- Neurologic examination findings (visual field deficits, cranial nerve abnormalities)
- Impact on work/ADLs: missed work days, disability
- For MS workup: clinical episodes, exam findings suggesting demyelination
- For tumor surveillance: prior imaging dates and findings

For NERVE CONDUCTION STUDIES / EMG (CPT 95910, 95907-95913):
- Distribution of symptoms (dermatomal vs stocking-glove vs focal)
- Duration of neuropathic symptoms (numbness, tingling, weakness, pain)
- Clinical exam findings: sensory loss pattern, motor weakness, reflex changes
- Differential diagnosis being evaluated
- Why electrodiagnostic study will change management
- Prior relevant imaging (spine MRI if radiculopathy suspected)
- Relevant labs (B12, glucose/A1c, TSH, ESR for neuropathy workup)
- Number of nerves/muscles to be tested with clinical justification

For SPINE MRI — CERVICAL/LUMBAR (CPT 72141, 72148):
- Radicular symptom pattern with dermatomal correlation
- Duration of symptoms and failed conservative treatment
- Neurologic deficits on examination
- Progressive neurologic symptoms warranting urgent imaging
- Prior imaging and interval change
- Red flags: myelopathy signs, progressive weakness, bowel/bladder changes

For EEG (CPT 95816, 95819):
- Seizure history: type, frequency, duration, witnesses
- Prior medications tried for seizure control
- Need for classification or medication adjustment
- Driving restrictions or safety concerns
- Spell characterization if diagnosis uncertain

## Payer-Specific Notes for Neurology PAs
- United Healthcare: Requires documented failure of 2+ migraine preventives before advanced imaging; strict on NCS medical necessity
- Aetna: References ACR Appropriateness Criteria explicitly; wants imaging to change management plan
- Cigna: Requires symptom duration thresholds before approving imaging; wants neurologic exam documented
- Humana: Generally approves MRI brain with adequate headache documentation
- BCBS: Varies significantly; focus on red flags and guideline adherence

## Common Denial Reasons and How to Preempt Them
1. "Routine headache — imaging not indicated" → Document red flags, failed treatments, change in pattern, or ICHD-3 criteria requiring workup
2. "NCS not medically necessary" → Specify how results will change management and list differential diagnosis
3. "Insufficient conservative treatment before MRI" → List all treatments with dates, doses, and why imaging is now needed to guide next steps
4. "Duplicate imaging" → Explain clinical change since prior study or why new study with different protocol is needed
