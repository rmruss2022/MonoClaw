SPECIALTY CONTEXT: GENERAL / CATCHALL
(Sleep studies, GI procedures, weight management medications, physical therapy, general imaging, DME)

## Key Clinical Guidelines to Reference
- AASM Clinical Guidelines for sleep disorders
- ACG/AGA Guidelines for GI procedures
- Endocrine Society / AGA Guidelines for obesity pharmacotherapy
- APTA Guidelines for physical therapy
- ACR Appropriateness Criteria for imaging

## General Documentation Points by Procedure Type

For SLEEP STUDIES / POLYSOMNOGRAPHY (CPT 95800):
- Symptoms: snoring, witnessed apneas, excessive daytime somnolence, morning headaches
- Epworth Sleepiness Scale (ESS) score
- STOP-BANG questionnaire score if available
- BMI and neck circumference
- Comorbidities affected by untreated OSA: hypertension, diabetes, atrial fibrillation, CHF, stroke risk
- Impact on safety: drowsy driving, occupational hazard
- Prior sleep testing results if retesting
- Home sleep test results if requesting in-lab study (document why in-lab needed: complex comorbidity, failed HSAT, suspected non-OSA disorder)

For GI PROCEDURES — EGD WITH BIOPSY (CPT 43239):
- Symptom duration and character (dysphagia, GERD symptoms, weight loss, anemia)
- Failed medical therapy: PPI trial duration and response (typically 4-8 weeks required)
- Alarm symptoms: unintentional weight loss, GI bleeding, dysphagia, persistent vomiting, iron deficiency anemia
- Screening indications: Barrett's surveillance intervals, family history
- Prior endoscopy findings if surveillance
- H. pylori testing and treatment history

For WEIGHT MANAGEMENT MEDICATIONS (Ozempic/Wegovy/semaglutide — J0274):
- BMI >= 30, or BMI >= 27 with weight-related comorbidity
- Weight-related comorbidities: type 2 diabetes, hypertension, dyslipidemia, OSA, NAFLD, PCOS
- Prior weight loss attempts: structured diet program, exercise program, behavioral counseling
- Duration of obesity/overweight
- Current diet and exercise regimen
- Prior weight management medications tried (if requesting specific agent)
- For semaglutide without diabetes: document FDA-approved indication for chronic weight management
- Contraindications to alternatives

For PHYSICAL THERAPY (CPT 97110, 97140, etc.):
- Specific diagnosis and functional limitations
- Objective measurements: ROM, strength, gait assessment
- Functional goals: return to work, ADL independence, pain reduction targets
- Treatment plan: modalities, frequency, expected duration
- Progress from prior PT if extending authorization
- Why continued PT is needed vs home exercise program
- Post-surgical PT: procedure date, surgeon's protocol, current phase of recovery

For SCREENING MAMMOGRAPHY (CPT 77067):
- Age and risk factors
- Family history (first-degree relatives with breast cancer)
- Personal history: prior biopsies, hormone use
- BRCA status if known
- Prior mammography results and BI-RADS category
- Screening guidelines met (USPSTF, ACS, ACR)

## Payer-Specific Notes for General PAs
- United Healthcare: Strict step therapy for GLP-1 agonists; requires documented behavioral program for weight management
- Aetna: Sleep study requires documented symptoms + ESS score; GLP-1 requires dietitian referral documentation
- Cigna: PT authorization in blocks; requires progress documentation for extensions; strict on weight management medication criteria
- Humana: Generally approves diagnostic studies with adequate clinical indication
- BCBS: Varies by plan; focus on medical necessity and guideline-concordant care

## Common Denial Reasons and How to Preempt Them
1. "Not FDA-approved indication" → Cite exact FDA indication and document patient meets criteria
2. "Step therapy required" → Document all prior formulary medications tried at adequate doses
3. "PT sessions exhausted" → Document measurable progress and remaining functional goals
4. "BMI criteria not met" → Document BMI calculation with height/weight and date, plus comorbidities
5. "Conservative treatment insufficient" → List all non-invasive approaches tried with specific outcomes
