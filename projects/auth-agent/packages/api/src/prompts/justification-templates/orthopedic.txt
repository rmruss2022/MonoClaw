SPECIALTY CONTEXT: ORTHOPEDIC PROCEDURES
(Knee arthroplasty, hip arthroplasty, spine surgery, arthroscopy, joint injections)

## Key Clinical Guidelines to Reference
- AAOS Clinical Practice Guidelines for knee/hip OA management
- ACR Appropriateness Criteria for musculoskeletal imaging
- NASS Evidence-Based Clinical Guidelines for spinal disorders
- AMA Guides to the Evaluation of Permanent Impairment

## Orthopedic-Specific Documentation Points

For JOINT REPLACEMENT (TKA/THA - CPT 27447, 27130):
- Kellgren-Lawrence radiographic grade (Grade III-IV typically required)
- Duration of conservative treatment (most payers require 3-6 months minimum)
- Specific conservative treatments attempted: PT, NSAIDs, corticosteroid injections, viscosupplementation, bracing
- Functional limitation: walking distance, stair climbing, ADL impacts
- BMI (many payers have BMI thresholds — document if within acceptable range or explain why surgery appropriate despite elevated BMI)
- Comorbidity optimization: diabetes control (A1c), smoking cessation, cardiac clearance
- Validated outcome scores if available: KOOS, WOMAC, Oxford Knee Score

For SPINE SURGERY (CPT 63030, 22612, 63650):
- Duration and type of conservative treatment (6-12 weeks PT typically required)
- Correlating imaging findings with clinical symptoms (dermatomal pattern)
- Neurologic deficits (motor weakness, sensory loss, reflex changes)
- Failed injection therapy (epidural steroid injections, facet blocks)
- Functional decline documentation
- For fusion: instability, spondylolisthesis grade, failed prior decompression
- For SCS: documented chronic pain >6 months, failed surgical intervention, psychological clearance

For IMAGING (MRI - CPT 72148, 72141, 70553):
- Document failed conservative treatment period (typically 4-6 weeks for most payers)
- Neurologic findings on exam (positive SLR, motor deficits, sensory changes)
- Red flag symptoms if present (progressive weakness, bowel/bladder dysfunction, saddle anesthesia)
- Prior imaging results if available
- Clinical indication that changes management

For INJECTIONS (CPT 64483, 64490):
- Specific pain location and distribution
- Duration of symptoms
- Failed oral medications (NSAIDs, muscle relaxants, neuropathic agents)
- Correlation of pain with imaging or exam findings
- Number of prior injections (most payers limit frequency)

## Payer-Specific Notes for Orthopedic PAs
- United Healthcare: Strict on PT duration requirements; wants specific number of sessions documented
- Aetna: Emphasize failure of conservative treatment with specific durations; references AAOS guidelines
- Cigna: Requires imaging correlation for spine procedures; wants functional limitation quantified
- Humana: Generally more lenient for joint replacement if radiographic criteria met
- BCBS: Varies by plan; focus on guideline adherence and functional impairment

## Common Denial Reasons and How to Preempt Them
1. "Insufficient conservative treatment" → Document every treatment with dates, frequency, and specific outcomes
2. "BMI too high" → Address BMI directly, cite studies showing benefit regardless of BMI, document optimization efforts
3. "Imaging does not support surgery" → Correlate specific imaging findings with symptoms and exam
4. "Missing PT records" → Reference PT program details including location, frequency, duration, and functional progress notes
