SPECIALTY CONTEXT: CARDIOLOGY PROCEDURES
(Stress tests, echocardiograms, cardiac catheterization, nuclear imaging)

## Key Clinical Guidelines to Reference
- AHA/ACC Appropriate Use Criteria for cardiac imaging
- ACC/AHA Guidelines for Exercise Testing
- ASNC Imaging Guidelines for nuclear cardiology
- ASE Guidelines for echocardiography

## Cardiology-Specific Documentation Points

For NUCLEAR CARDIAC STRESS TEST (CPT 78452):
- Pre-test probability of CAD (use ACC/AHA risk stratification)
- Cardiac risk factors: hypertension, diabetes, hyperlipidemia, smoking, family history, obesity
- Symptom description: chest pain characteristics (typical angina, atypical, non-cardiac), exertional dyspnea
- Functional class (NYHA or CCS angina class)
- Resting ECG findings (if abnormal, document why standard exercise treadmill is insufficient)
- Reason for pharmacologic vs exercise stress (functional limitation, LBBB, pacemaker, beta-blocker use)
- Prior cardiac testing and results
- Why stress testing will change management (medication adjustment, referral for catheterization)

For ECHOCARDIOGRAM (CPT 93306):
- Clinical indication: new murmur, heart failure symptoms, valve disease monitoring
- Symptoms: dyspnea, edema, syncope, palpitations with hemodynamic concern
- Physical exam: S3/S4, murmur grade and characteristics, JVD, peripheral edema
- BNP/NT-proBNP levels if available
- Prior echo findings if surveillance study (valve gradients, EF, chamber size)
- Interval change in symptoms since last study
- How results will guide medication or surgical management

For CARDIAC CATHETERIZATION (CPT 93458, 93459):
- Non-invasive test results suggesting significant ischemia
- Symptoms refractory to optimal medical therapy
- Risk stratification results (high-risk Duke treadmill score, large perfusion defect)
- LVEF if known
- Prior revascularization history
- ACS presentation details if applicable

For VASCULAR STUDIES — DUPLEX (CPT 93971):
- Symptoms: leg swelling, pain, skin changes
- Risk factors for DVT: recent surgery, immobility, cancer, prior DVT
- Wells score or clinical probability assessment
- D-dimer results if obtained
- Physical exam findings: unilateral edema, calf tenderness, Homans sign

## Payer-Specific Notes for Cardiology PAs
- United Healthcare: Follows AUC criteria closely; requires risk factor documentation and pre-test probability assessment
- Aetna: Wants documentation of why non-invasive testing first; references ACC/AHA appropriateness criteria
- Cigna: Requires intermediate or high pre-test probability for stress testing; wants functional limitation documented
- Humana: Generally approves with adequate symptom and risk factor documentation
- BCBS: Emphasize guideline-concordant indications; document that results will change management

## Common Denial Reasons and How to Preempt Them
1. "Low pre-test probability" → Document all risk factors, calculate and state Framingham or ACC risk score
2. "Routine screening not covered" → Emphasize this is diagnostic, not screening — cite specific symptoms and clinical concern
3. "Repeat study too soon" → Document interval change in symptoms or clinical status since last study
4. "Exercise treadmill sufficient" → Explain why nuclear/pharmacologic is needed (abnormal baseline ECG, functional limitation, prior equivocal results)
