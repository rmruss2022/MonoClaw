SPECIALTY CONTEXT: BEHAVIORAL HEALTH
(Psychiatric medications, psychotherapy, psychological testing, substance abuse treatment)

## Key Clinical Guidelines to Reference
- APA Practice Guidelines for psychiatric disorders
- AACAP Guidelines (pediatric/adolescent)
- ASAM Criteria for substance use treatment levels
- VA/DoD Clinical Practice Guidelines for PTSD, depression
- FDA-approved indications for psychiatric medications

## Behavioral Health-Specific Documentation Points

For PSYCHIATRIC MEDICATION AUTHORIZATION (e.g., Esketamine/Spravato, atypical antipsychotics, stimulants):
- Primary psychiatric diagnosis with DSM-5 criteria met
- Validated screening scores: PHQ-9 (depression), GAD-7 (anxiety), AUDIT-C (alcohol), PCL-5 (PTSD), MDQ (bipolar)
- Current symptom severity and functional impairment
- COMPLETE medication trial history:
  - Each medication name, dose, duration (minimum adequate trial = typically 4-8 weeks at therapeutic dose)
  - Reason for discontinuation (ineffective, side effects, contraindicated)
  - Document at least 2-3 adequate trials in the same class before requesting non-formulary
- Current medication regimen and compliance
- Therapy history (type, frequency, duration, response)
- Suicidal ideation assessment (Columbia Scale or similar)
- Substance use screening
- For controlled substances: prescription drug monitoring program (PDMP) check, urine drug screen

For PSYCHOTHERAPY (CPT 90837 — 60-minute individual therapy):
- DSM-5 diagnosis with clinical justification
- Treatment modality and evidence base (CBT, DBT, EMDR, etc.)
- Treatment goals: specific, measurable, achievable
- Current symptom severity (validated scales)
- Frequency requested and clinical rationale
- Progress from prior sessions if continuing care
- Why this level/frequency of therapy is needed vs lower intensity
- Functional impairment: work, relationships, ADLs, safety

For PSYCHOLOGICAL TESTING (CPT 96130-96133):
- Referral question and diagnostic uncertainty
- Why clinical interview alone is insufficient for diagnosis
- Specific tests planned and clinical rationale for each
- Number of testing hours requested with justification
- How results will change treatment plan
- Prior testing results and why retesting is needed

For SUBSTANCE ABUSE TREATMENT:
- ASAM criteria level determination
- Current and historical substance use pattern
- Prior treatment episodes and outcomes
- Withdrawal risk assessment
- Co-occurring psychiatric diagnoses
- Medical complications of substance use
- Social determinants affecting recovery

## Payer-Specific Notes for Behavioral Health PAs
- United Healthcare (Optum Behavioral): Step therapy required for most psych meds; requires PHQ-9/GAD-7 scores; therapy auth in blocks of 10-20 sessions
- Aetna: Requires failed formulary alternatives before non-formulary approval; wants measurable treatment goals
- Cigna (Evernorth): Reviews therapy necessity every 20 sessions; wants documented progress toward goals
- Humana: Generally approves initial therapy course; stricter on medication step therapy
- BCBS: Varies by plan; focus on medical necessity and treatment plan with goals

## Common Denial Reasons and How to Preempt Them
1. "Step therapy not completed" → Document EVERY prior medication trial with dose, duration, and outcome — even brief trials or those from other providers
2. "Not medically necessary at this frequency" → Cite severity scores, safety concerns, functional impairment requiring intensive treatment
3. "Testing hours excessive" → Justify each test battery component and estimated time; explain diagnostic complexity
4. "Outpatient sufficient" → If requesting higher level of care, document why outpatient is unsafe or insufficient using ASAM or LOCUS criteria
