2. Polyneuropathy, unspecified — G62.9
Requesting nerve conduction study and electromyography (NCS/EMG) of bilateral lower extremities (CPT 95910) to characterize the type, severity, and distribution of peripheral neuropathy.

MEDICAL NECESSITY:
Electrodiagnostic testing is medically necessary to objectively characterize this patient's progressive peripheral neuropathy. While diabetic polyneuropathy is the most likely etiology, NCS/EMG is needed to confirm the diagnosis, determine whether the neuropathy is axonal, demyelinating, or mixed, assess severity to guide management intensity, and exclude superimposed conditions such as chronic inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy (CIDP) or lumbosacral radiculopathy, which would require different treatment. The patient has progressive symptoms, functional impairment (falls risk, Romberg positive), and loss of protective sensation placing him at high risk for foot ulceration and amputation. Electrodiagnostic data will directly inform treatment planning, glycemic targets, and preventive foot care protocols.
