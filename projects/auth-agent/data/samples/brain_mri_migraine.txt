MRI Brain with and without contrast is medically necessary to exclude secondary pathology in a patient with chronic migraine whose aura pattern has undergone a clinically significant change, now including hemisensory symptoms and transient expressive aphasia. This is consistent with AAN/AHS practice guidelines recommending imaging when headache pattern changes or new neurological features develop.
